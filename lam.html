HO CHI MINH AND HIS EARLY LIFE

Ho Chi Minh (when Nguyen Sinh Cung was young, Nguyen Tat Thanh was in school years, many years of revolutionary activity named Nguyen Ai Quoc and many other pseudonyms and pseudonyms) was born on May 19, 1890 in Kim Lien Commune, Nam Dan District, Nghe An Province; died on September 2, 1969 in Hanoi.

Chi Minh was born into a patriotic family, growing up in a locality with a brave tradition against foreign invaders. Living in a country under the yoke of French colonialism, his childhood and youth, Ho Chi Minh witnessed the tribulations of his compatriots and anti-colonialist movements, Ho Chi Minh Soon, the colonialists will be exterminated, the independence for the country will be brought to bring the freedom and happiness to the people.

Ho Chi Minh's revolutionary road:

In June 1911, Ho Chi Minh left the country to go to the West to find the path of national liberation.

From 1912 to 1917, under the name of Nguyen Tat Thanh, Ho Chi Minh came to many countries in Asia, Europe, America, Africa, living with the working people. Through practice, Ho Chi Minh deeply felt the hard life of the working people and the colonial peoples as well as their spiritual aspirations. Ho Chi Minh soon became aware of the struggle for national liberation of the Vietnamese people as part of the common struggle of the people of the world and actively worked to unite the peoples of the peoples in the struggle. independence, freedom. In late 1917, Ho Chi Minh returned to France from France, working in the Viet Kieu movement and the French workers movement.

In 1919, Ho Chi Minh sent to the Versailles Conference the claim of freedom for the Vietnamese people and the right to self for the people of the colonies.

The Claims by Ho Chi Minh. Sent in 1919.

Nguyen Ai Quoc participated in the 18th Congress of the Socialist Party of France


In October 1923, at the First Farmers International Conference, Nguyen Ai Quoc was elected to the International Farmers Council.

In November 1924, as a member of the Communist International Committee of the Communist Party and member of the International Farmer Union, Nguyen Ai Quoc arrived in Guangzhou (China).

In 1925, Nguyen Ai Quoc established the Vietnam Revolutionary Youth Association, directly organized training courses for revolutionary cadres, launched the weekly "Youth", the first revolutionary newspaper of Vietnam to spread the master Marxism - Leninism in Vietnam, preparation for the establishment of the Communist Party of Vietnam.

In February 1930, Nguyen Ai Quoc chaired a meeting to establish a party in Cuu Long, Hong Kong (China). The Congress approved the shortened, brief regulations of the Communist Party of Vietnam.


From 1934 to 1938, Nguyen Ai Quoc studied at the Institute for the Study of Colonial Ethnic Issues in Moscow (Soviet Union).

In October 1938, he left the Soviet Union to China, contacted the party organization preparing to return home.

January 28, 1941, Nguyen Ai Quoc returned home after more than 30 years away from the country.

In August 1942, named for Ho Chi Minh, the representative of the Viet Minh Front and the Vietnam Chapter of the International Association Against Invasion to China sought international alliance.

In September 1944, Ho Chi Minh returned to Cao Bang base. In December 1944, Ho Chi Minh ordered the establishment of the Vietnamese Liberation Army, the precursor of the Vietnam People's Army.

In August 1945, Ho Chi Minh and the Central Party led the people's uprising to win the victory.

On September 2, 1945, at the Ba Dinh Square in Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh read "Declaration of Independence", declared the establishment of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam and became the first president of Vietnam South independent.

After that, the French colonialists committed war, plotted to invade Vietnam again.

In February 1951, at the second National Congress of the Party, he was elected Chairman of the Vietnam Labor Party. Under the leadership of the Central Committee and President Ho Chi Minh, the resistance of the Vietnamese people against the French colonialists won a great victory, gloriously ending with the historic victory of Dien Bien Phu (1954). ), completely liberated the North.

Since 1954, the People's Commissar of the Labor Party of Vietnam led the people to build socialism in the North and to fight for the liberation of the South, unifying the country.

In September 1960, at the Third National Party Congress, the person elected as the Chairman of the Central Committee of the Party, the President of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam.

In 1964, the US imperialists launched a war of air destruction by destroying North Vietnam. Uncle Ho and the people determined to defeat the American invaders.

September 2, 1969, He died in Hanoi.

Before his death, President Ho Chi Minh left the Vietnamese people a historical testament, telling the Vietnamese people what to do to build the country after the war, hoping for the whole party and all the people. To build a peaceful, unified, independent, democratic and prosperous country, to make a worthy contribution to the revolutionary cause of the world.

In the spring of 1975, with the historic Ho Chi Minh Campaign, the Vietnamese people completed the liberation of the South, reunified the Fatherland, and made the holy wish of President Ho Chi Minh.

In 1987, the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) honored Ho Chi Minh as "Hero of National Liberation, Vietnam's Outstanding Cultural House."

After all, President Ho Chi Minh was the great teacher of the Vietnamese revolution, the beloved leader of the working class and the whole Vietnamese nation, an outstanding soldier, a brilliant activist. international communist movement and national liberation movement. He applied and developed Marxism-Leninism to the specific conditions of Vietnam, founded the Communist Party of Vietnam, founded the Vietnam National United Front, founded the People's Armed Forces Vietnam and the founding of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam (now the Socialist Republic of Vietnam). He always attached the Vietnamese revolution to the common struggle of the people of the world for peace, national independence, democracy and social progress.
